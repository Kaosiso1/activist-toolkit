The Black Girl Code
Colorism and misrepresentation: The many struggles of being a black woman.

Background

Colorism: prejudice or discrimination against individuals with a dark skin tone, typically among people of the same ethnic or racial group. Misrepresentation: the action or offense of giving a false or misleading account of the nature of something.

Colorism

Black women and girls are constantly being disrespected and ridiculed among the black community, especially from our own men, mainly through the act of colorism. Because of this, black women may tend to feel unworthy, unloved, and garner extreme insecurities that result in self hate, solely at the fault of the color of our skin and black features (hair, skin, lips, nose).

.

Misrepresentation

Misrepresentation of black women and girls include what we see the media portray us as today. On TV, black women are seen as masculine, aggressive, and less than compared to the other characters of the show--it’s a harmful stereotype. And whenever they do portray black women in a positive light, the show/movie fail to cast an actual black woman, instead they cast a woman that could “pass” as black, when in reality they are biracial (ex. Zendaya, Halle Berry, Amandla Stenberg, Yara Shahidi).

.

Black women make up only 8% of the population, but in 2005 accounted for 22% of the intimate partner homicide victims and 29% of all female victims of intimate partner homicide. The majority of black women also experience sexual violence. Approximately 40% of black women report coercive contact of a sexual nature by age 18.
